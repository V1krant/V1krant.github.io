What is Emotional Intelligence?

A quote from Google "the capacity to be aware of, control, and express one's emotions, and to handle interpersonal relationships judiciously and empathetically.".

I personally find EQ more important than IQ most of the time unless your job requires you to be smart. I've listed what Daniel Goleman, an American pychologist think the five key elements of EQ are:

Self-awareness.
Self-regulation.
Motivation.
Empathy.
Social skills.

How is it different to IQ?

I think Healthline.com answers this perfectly with a quote from there site and I changed it a bit "IQ is your ability to solve problems, use logic, and grasp or communicate complex ideas. EQ is your ability to recognize emotion in yourself and others, and to use that awareness to guide your decisions"

Why is it important?

Because helps you deal with your emotional state and how you deal with people on the daily.
